Rays¶

Class definition & methods¶

class murasyp.rays.Ray(data)[source]¶

Rays are immutable, hashable rational-valued with max-norm one.

param:a mapping (such as a dict) to Rational values, i.e., Fraction. The fractions may be specified by giving an int, a float or in their str-representation. Not all values may be zero.
type:Mapping

This class derives from Gamble, so its methods apply here as well. What has changed:

  • the max-norm is one;
  • the support becomes the domain.
>>> Ray(Gamble({'a': 5, 'b': -1, 'c': 0}))
Ray({'a': 1, 'b': '-1/5'})

Also, arithmetic with rays results in gambles (which can of course be converted to rays).

>>> r = Ray({'a': 1,'b': -2})
>>> r / 2
Gamble({'a': '1/4', 'b': '-1/2'})
>>> Ray(r * r)
Ray({'a': '1/4', 'b': 1})
